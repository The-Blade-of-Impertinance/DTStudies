Definition

Technology push occurs when advances in science, materials, or manufacturing lead to the development of new products. The innovation comes from the manufacturer or researcher, not in response to consumer demand — the technology itself creates the market.

How it works
Research leads.
Products follow.
A new material or process is discovered, then manufacturers find ways to commercialise it into products consumers didn't ask for but quickly adopt.

How technology push works

From lab discovery to consumer product

The process

Technology push follows a supply-led chain: a new capability is developed, then manufacturers and designers work out how to turn it into a sellable product. Consumer desire comes after the product exists.

1

Scientific or material breakthrough

Researchers discover a new material, process, or technology — e.g. graphene, LED technology, or carbon fibre composites.

2

Potential applications identified

Engineers and designers explore how the new capability could be used in products, even if no customer has requested it.

3

Product is developed and launched

A product is manufactured and brought to market. Marketing then works to create demand for something consumers didn't previously know they wanted.

4

Consumer adoption

The public begins using the product and it may become essential — like smartphones, which pushed entirely new industries into existence.

Key point for your exam

Technology push is innovation-led. The question to ask is: "Did the technology come before the demand?" If yes, it is technology push.

Pattern to spot

Technology push examples often follow the pattern: research or accident → discovery → product application. Nobody asked for Velcro — it was invented first, then marketed.

Impacts of technology push

Social, economic and environmental effects

Positive impacts
+

Improved quality of life

New materials and technologies create products that solve problems people didn't know could be solved — e.g. MRI scanners, lightweight prosthetics.

+

Economic growth

New industries and jobs are created around emerging technologies, boosting GDP and creating skilled employment.

+

More sustainable materials

Technology push has produced alternatives to harmful materials — e.g. biodegradable plastics, solar cell advances.

Negative impacts
–

Planned obsolescence

Rapid tech advancement encourages consumers to discard working products for newer ones, increasing electronic waste.

–

Job displacement

Automation driven by technology push can replace workers, particularly in manufacturing.

–

Unequal access

New technology is often expensive at launch, meaning less affluent communities benefit later — widening the inequality gap.

Extension answer — what to look for

A good answer names the specific technology, explains that it was developed before any consumer demand existed, and states that manufacturers then found a product application for it. Mentioning that marketing had to create the demand afterwards gets top marks.
